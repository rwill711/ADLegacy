Adventure Legacy is a 2.5d Tactics Game, using GODOT 4.6.2:
Key Points:
-Clone of Final Fantasy Tactics Advanced, including job system, map-tile placing system
-Additionally we will implement attacks and skills that interact w/ the environment 
-Main piece of balancing will be a CPU tracking system that will allow the NPCs to adapt to your play style, called "FOIL" system. There should be a tracker in the background that keeps note of your most used attacks and breaks them into archetype 'playstyles' that the computer will adapt to play against. To start; we will have level 0-4 with 0 being no adaptation and 4 being the CPU is tailored to beat your most common tactics. For example; if you are a fire mage character and in all of your encounters you have used fire spells, the computer will at level 1 have a fire resistance potion that makes the attacks less effective, at level 4 the enemies would be equipped with armor that negates fire damage. 

Game is also rogue-like, with death being a main part used to push the story forward. The player will leave behind 'legacies' based on how much renown they accumulate (renown is detailed more in creative log). For example, if you are a great warrior and you 'retire' with a lot of renown, you might be able to establish a statue in a city and leave a piece of armor or a weapon at the location w/ a requirement for a certain amount of renown to remove it. The next character then has a 'Legacy' quest that can allow them to achieve just that. Characters with enough renown can also have 'legacy' characters that inherit specific skills, or have increased stats based on their predecessor. Almost like a New Game+ concept as it goes on. But the weapons and armor you unlock or forge will also be available to the CPU as time goes on, or a unique weapon you made might fall into the hands of the person who defeated you, which will then be able to be looted if you defeat them in the next life.

CHUNK 4 — foil_analyzer.gd DETAILS:
  - build_profile(tracker, character_name, trait_tags) → FOILProfile
  - Trait tags permanently vote for archetypes (TRAIT_WEIGHT_PER_TAG = 0.35)
      even when rolling window is empty — keeps identity from evaporating
      during long streaks of odd playstyle. Safe to call with no battle data.
  - Category weights normalized to 0–1 by total action count.
  - Archetype weights normalized so max archetype = 1.0.
  - AOE-heavy players get an AOE_BLASTER boost scaled by aoe_tendency;
      prevents a mage who only casts Fireball from being flagged pure MAGIC_OFFENSE.
  - Dominant archetype flips to HYBRID if the top two are within HYBRID_MARGIN
      (0.12) of each other; avoids flip-flopping the CPU's counter target.
  - Confidence ramps linearly from 0 at MIN_BATTLES to 1.0 at ROLLING_WINDOW_SIZE.
      Below MIN_BATTLES confidence is hard 0 and the loadout builder uses FOIL 0.
  - resolve_foil_level(renown, mission_override) — single helper so BattleManager
      doesn't have to care whether the current battle is a story override or free.

CHUNK 5 — foil_loadout_builder.gd DETAILS:
  - build_enemy_team(profile, foil_level, base_enemy_pool, party_size) → Dictionary
  - Return shape:
      {
        "foil_level": int,
        "dominant_archetype": int,
        "enemies": Array[Dictionary],  # each: {job, role, consumable, gear_hint, ...}
        "ai_hints": Dictionary,        # {target_priority, formation, engagement_range, ...}
        "notes": Array[String],        # audit log, useful for dev overlay
      }
  - Level-by-level layering mirrors ADR-003 exactly:
        0 base pool
        1 + counter consumables
        2 + half team swapped to counter roles (interleaves top-2 archetypes
            for clear hybrid players so the enemy team isn't one-note)
        3 + team AI hints (target_priority, formation, engagement_range, defensive_posture)
        4 + full team swap + elite_ prefixed consumables + counter gear hints
  - Graceful fallback: null profile, FOIL 0, or insufficient data → returns the
      base-pool configuration with notes explaining why.
  - Symbolic (string-tag) output on purpose — job/equipment/element systems
      aren't ported yet; EnemySpawner will translate hints → real JobData/ItemData
      when those come online. See EnemySpawner contract below.

ENEMY-SPAWNER CONTRACT (for when it's built):
  Input:  the Dictionary returned by FOILLoadoutBuilder.build_enemy_team().
  Responsibilities:
    - For each entry in config["enemies"]:
        if "role" is set and is in COUNTER_JOBS_BY_ARCHETYPE, pick a real
          JobData from the region's enemy pool that fulfills that role;
          otherwise use entry["job"] directly.
        if "consumable" is set, grant one stack at battle start.
          "elite_*" prefix means use the tier-2 variant.
        if "gear_hint" is set, swap appropriate equipment slot to the closest
          match from the enemy pool; fall back silently if no match.
    - Pass config["ai_hints"] to each enemy's AI controller on spawn.
    - Log config["notes"] to the dev overlay when a debug flag is on.
